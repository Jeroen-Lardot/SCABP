THE BEER-HIIT STUDY

THE IMPACT OF ALCOHOL ON HIGH-INTENSITY INTERVAL PERFORMANCE

March 19, 2021 Jieh-Jang Chen PODCAST
  • Beer-HIIT Study Q&A
  • Jieh-Jang Chen
  • 5:34
  • Beer-HIIT Study Q&A
  • Jieh-Jang Chen
  • / 5:34

The importance of physical exercise has become more important to humans in the 21st century, as majority of people now work in offices and sit in front of a computer nearly all the time. With the pandemic upon us, one may be consider building an outgoing and physical lifestyle 

High intensity interval workout or HIIT is an excellent option for people who are busy and don’t have much time for long hours of workout. A typical HIIT workout ranges from only 30-60 mins, however the result in the improvement of body composition, (by reducing fat mass (FM) and increasing muscle mass) is much more effective than moderate-intensity continuous training for longer period of time. 

Beer, one of the world favourite drinks, is also known to be high in calories, but also reports have shown that alcohol can inhibit muscle protein synthesis. Researchers have therefore set out to investigate the influence of moderate consumption of beer and HIIT on body composition in close to real life scenarios.

The experiment 

The researchers recruited 72 healthy adults (age 18-40) divided in to five groups, 14 which to not take part in any training, 13 with had regular HIIT training and beer intake, 15 which had regular HIIT training and 0% alcohol beer intake, 16 with had regular HIIT training and water intake, and 14 with had regular HIIT training with sparkling water with added vodka ethanol intake.  

HIIT training consisted of 2 days per week with 48 hr in-between, and every session lasting 40-65 min. Regular amount of beverage intake was 660mL for men and 330mL for women everyday from Monday to Friday. After 10 weeks, the participants were assessed in anthropometric parameters and body composition 

Results 

The primary finding and also the answer to whether moderate beer or alcohol intake will affect HIIT training is no. From this experiment, for every participant in the four HIIT training group all resulted in significantly reduced fat mass, fat mass percentage, and increase truck lean mass (muscle mass). However, body mass was not largely affected, since the reduced fat mass was nearly equal to the increased muscle mass. This is in cohesion with other studies showing that HIIT does not necessary change BMI in normal weight individuals, but however does effectively reduce fat mass and increase muscle mass.

Hence this means good news for any fellow beer or alcohol enthusiasts who also want to use HIIT for healthier lifestyle or improve physique. You do not need to give up beer in order to have the full benefit of HIIT training. However, one should note, that this experiment was done on moderate beer and alcohol intake, the effects of large amount of beer or alcohol might not be the same. In addition, the experiment was done on healthy individuals age 18-40, so studies for people who maybe be older or some health issues still need to be done. For a normal person, HIIT is still highly recommended with scientific evidence to back it up, and can even be done with regular beer intake, so a big bonus!
